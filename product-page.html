CHALLENGE

I worked with The Washington Post’s conversion team on subscription acquisition touchpoints. Subscriptions make up the majority of the paper's revenue, but their numbers had been in decline (8.32% Y/Y in 2022), reaching a low of approximately 2.5 million subscriptions. Sentiment research suggested that users found the page boring and confusing while analytics reported stagnant product page to checkout conversion rates in 2022, at an average of 8.53%. The product page hadn’t changed in a major way in five years.

SOLUTION

I led a project to increase user engagement and improve conversions on the product page with bold new designs without erasing past wins. I conducted discovery research, design solutioning, and usability testing before prioritizing and A/B testing four hypotheses:


  1. Visual overhaul
  2. One-click CTA & stronger features
  3. Simplified billing copy
  4. FAQ section
IMPACT

21%

increase in subscription conversions compared to control

$2.74M

year-one revenue impact of this design improvement
