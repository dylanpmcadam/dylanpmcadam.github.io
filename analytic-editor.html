Introduction

OVERVIEW

Govini’s Decision Science Platform is a data analytics web app used throughout the U.S. government to better direct investments and make decisions. Government personnel rely on analytics that condense the data found in this platform to communicate key insights to their teams.


(Disclaimer: in the context of this case study the term “analytic” refers to any data visualization.)

PROBLEM

We were losing users with our incomplete analytic workflow. Our users were being forced to leave the platform to complete tasks in the following ways:


  1. Exporting our data to create analytics in other software.

  2. Exporting images of our default analytics to customize the colors, title, etc.
SOLUTION

Our vision was to create a feature that contributed to making the platform a complete workspace, rather than just a data export tool. We would accomplish this by releasing an analytic editor, allowing users to:


  1. Create analytics in-platform from our data.

  2. Customize analytic appearance and order.
GOAL

Our ongoing focus was to get users to stay longer and accomplish more within the platform. We aimed to solve issues with analytic workflows by releasing this feature before the end of Q2 2021.

We believed that the Decision Science Platform would be utilized more if government employees could successfully create and edit analytics within the platform. We hoped this feature would also increase our key engagement metric (Weekly Active Users) and strengthen our sales pitch.

Research

PAIN POINTS

Understanding the problems with our users' current analytic workflow was key to determining our solution. Past research had shown that we have 2 main types of users: creators and consumers.

Our creator persona, dubbed GS-9 Jerry, is a typical government analyst who needs to create content to aid in national security decision making. After reviewing feedback, requirements, and solutions with users matching this persona, two main pain points emerged:


  • “The data searches I run don’t give me what I need. I have to export the data, bring it into Tableau, and create charts there. This really slows me down.”

  • “I literally have to screenshot the default analytics and go into Microsoft Paint to change the colors or paste over the legends or add titles.”

USABILITY TESTING

I created a clickable prototype that I tested with internal users to ensure that the UI and flow were comprehensible. Before the release I ran a week of user acceptance testing (UAT) with the product manager to ensure the developed feature matched our intended use cases, while also checking for styling errors or bugs.

FEATURES

Analytic Types:
Create or edit one of ten analytic types.

Variables:
Variables are surfaced to control what data is visualized. Nivo.rocks, a React d3 library, provided the bulk of this functionality.

Reorder:
Curate the order of your analytics before exporting the page as a PDF for use in reports or presentations.

Insights

OBSTACLES

During UAT testing, I discovered we lacked a variable input reset. So if “Year” was selected for the x-axis of a scatter plot, there wasn’t a way to clear this choice. The engineering team and I worked ad-hoc to solve this issue by adding a “None” option to the input dropdown. While this addition wasn’t in the initial release, it was prioritized for the following sprint.

Real-world constraints ultimately determine a feature’s fate:


  • I completed the design for this analytic editor feature in the Fall of 2020. The project was then reprioritized for 2021, resulting in a complete redesign for new requirements.

  • A lack of engineering bandwidth caused half of the workflow to be descoped after dev handoff. While the wasted effort was unfortunate, it allowed the feature to be delivered on time.
SUCCESS METRICS

Despite bugs found by our QA team that pushed this release to the beginning of Q3, this feature is now live in the Decision Science Platform.

We’ve seen an increase by 43% in users accessing analytics attached to their data searches. Additionally, our Weekly Active Users (WAU) has grown by 16% and 12% following the release of analytic editor.
