Govini Design System

Company-wide source of truth

TIMELINE

August 2020 - July 2021

ROLE

Design System Lead

COMPANY

Govini

TEAM

UX: Ellis Lee, Vishal Kanwar, Hannah Faub / Engineering: Kelley McCahill, Nate Chapman, Chris McKay

Introduction

OVERVIEW

I lead the year-long effort with our design and engineering teams to create a design system for Govini’s Decision Science Platform, a government-focused data analytics web app.

PROBLEM
  1. Our design resources and platform were managed haphazardly as priorities shifted. We reinvented the wheel and missed inconsistencies too often.

  2. Our data-heavy use cases had bloated the functions of our platform, causing it to look outdated and complex.

  3. Shared knowledge was stored in the brains of individual contributors. When they went on vacation or left the company, our whole team suffered.
SOLUTION
  1. Retool our design library by auditing our platform and recreating all our components.

  2. Reskin our platform to look modern and trustworthy while implementing design tokens to standardize styling.

  3. Launch a documentation website to serve as a source of design truth for the entire company.
GOAL

Our new design system would pay off if we could make our product development process quicker and less prone to error. Additionally, our reskin aimed to improve user impressions of our platform and company.

Preparation

INSPIRATION

I researched other published design systems to gain a sense of the most common elements and organizational strategies.

Numerous components in our codebase relied on the Material UI React Framework. Our redesigned system had to fit within the constraints of this framework, while making room for our own brand and use cases.

UI INVENTORY

I began by cataloguing all the variations in our design files and platform. Viewed side-by-side, it was easy to spot duplicate components and inconsistent styling.

COMPANY BUY-IN

Design systems are often perceived by others as a ‘design thing’. As a result, we first focused on selling our executives on how a new design system would benefit all teams. Relying on our UI inventory to tell the visual story of our current shortcomings helped immensely.

Design

SITE MAP

I created a 43-page site map for our platform, consisting of screenshots or actual mockups of each screen. This gave us an indispensable overview, allowing us to gauge the connections between different features and determine which screens weren’t represented in our design files.

DESIGN LIBRARY

I recreated our existing 1-page Sketch design library in Figma, building an additional 400+ components from scratch. I expanded our library by including edge cases like states (empty, error, etc.), interaction states, spacing, layout grids, and responsive guidelines.

RESKIN

To modernize the look of our platform, we overhauled key aspects like color, typography, shadows, navigation, and headers with an eye for consistency and subtlety.

DESIGN TOKENS

We used this opportunity to integrate design tokens for typography, color, and shadows into our codebase, keeping our design and development more consistent. I worked closely with the development team on a process using Diez for converting Figma design standards into typescript files for the development team’s translation to SASS styling variables.

DOCUMENTATION WEBSITE

A website documenting our design system was launched in a company-wide meeting. Our guidelines section provided details on our philosophy, process, and accessibility standards. Each component section included usage information and inspect functionality.

Insights

LESSONS

A design system is a living tool that isn't built overnight. This continues to be an emergent result of cycles of communication and maintenance.

Chunking out changes to coincide with relevant projects helped build momentum. For example, typography tokens might be included in one feature while a header refactor would be updated across the platform in a later release.

The design system has had an immediate impact on the speed of our product development, allowing both of our product teams to add roughly 8-10 points per sprint. Designers can limit decision paralysis by relying on pre-built styles and components while developers can reduce the number of handoff meetings needed as they depend on design tokens and usage guidelines.

FUTURE

We are waiting on Q3 Net Promotor Score (NPS) feedback to gauge the effect our platform reskin had on user perceptions.

Our design system continues to be limited without a code library. We have begun working with the engineering team to replicate our components in Storybook's front-end library.
